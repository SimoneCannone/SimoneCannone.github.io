About the Project

Semi-professional final year video game project carried out together with 7 programmers, 7 artists and 2 designers. Made in Unreal 5 within 9 months.

And published on steam : https://store.steampowered.com/app/2999250/Katto_Rising_Tides/

My main work was the implementation of the enemy at range (Volitenus) in addition to the sound system of the game in which I implemented a sound system which allowed the use of layering within Unreal. Apart from that, I helped my colleagues with optimization and error correction tasks.

Main Tasks

Volitenus is the game's flying turret enemy. When it detects you, it aligns itself with you to shoot. Its shots will try to predict the character's movement slightly. Additionally, it cannot shoot at the character if they are directly above or below it. To eliminate it, you must jump over it, and upon its defeat, it will propel you in the opposite direction from which you approached.

For the sound system, I used Unreal's base tools, but only the basic audio player. The entire system for handling multi-channel audio was built from scratch, allowing greater flexibility and control over how sounds are played and managed. Additionally, I developed a complete system for fading individual tracks or sets of tracks, ensuring smooth transitions between different audio layers. This was all achieved using only Unreal's built-in audio players without relying on external middleware. On top of that, I also implemented all the game's sound effects (SFX), carefully designing and integrating them to enhance the overall audio experience.

Main Features

Platform Game

A 3D platformer inspired by classics like Prince of Persia and Mario Bros.With a variety of enemies and locations.

Layering Sound

Using a layered sound system that allows for a better progression experience for the player.

Unreal 5

Game made in Unreal 5, taking full advantage of its capabilities, in order to achieve maximum performance in the game and very beautiful graphics.

Playable on steam deck

Wide range of optimization which allows us to perform tests and verify that the game was mostly stable in a Steam Deck environment.
